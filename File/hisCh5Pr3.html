History of the Empire - Chapter 5.3, The end of the Second Big War

Mal’s betrayal had big consequences. Discontent among its populace, angered by the government’s duplicity, transformed into protests. By 224 p.I., Mal’s ruling regime faced a full-scale revolution, with reformist factions seizing power and seeking to rejoin the Aspakeian-Sarkan alliance. The new government, under pressure to prove its loyalty, provided vital intelligence on Kojkan and Lamnan movements, significantly aiding the allied war effort. In 223 p.I., with Kojkos and Lamna on the edge of collapse, peace negotiations began. Held in Lucozhkei, the talks were mediated by the newly reformed government of Mal. The Pact of Lucozhkei stated that Kojkos and Lamna were forced to cede contested territories to Aspakei and Sark. Being victorious, they chose the lands to annex, and decided that they needed more plains and fertile areas, so they annexed the region of Dhoynkyo, now part of Aspozhmonto. Also, both aggressor states agreed to significantly reduce their military capabilities, and were forced to pay reparations to Aspakei and Sark.

Keyko retired permanently, leaving a legacy of military excellence and strategic brilliance. Aspakei’s government, wary of future conflicts, established the Coalition Defence Council, a permanent body to oversee regional security and prevent the rise of aggressor states.
