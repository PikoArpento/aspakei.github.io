History of the Empire - Chapter 2.2, external policy

Of the 44 independent countries at the start of the First Civil War, excluding Aspakei and The Kingdom of Elu, only half recognize Aspakei, 15 recognize only The Kingdom, and 8 recognize both. Of those in favour of the Republic of Aspakei, the important Republics of Tehera and Mal, two of the strongest nations at the time. At the vote for recognition at the International Congress, Aspakei is officially recognized on December 15, 397 p.I. with 70% of the votes in favour, and officially becomes part of it five days later.

With the officialization of the independence of the Republic of Aspakei, diplomatic efforts to ensure a lasting peace between all nations began, so on January 1, 385 BC, Aspakei together with the Republic of Tehera and the Republic of Mal, signed the Treaty of the Republics. The treaty was both a defensive and offensive pact in case of war and an agreement to unite the three nations as much as possible.

In summary, it provides that militarily that those present would always help each other respecting humanitarian laws, legally that those present would always maintain a democracy and recognize the same inviolable rights for all, and economically that those present would always support each other and that the Taro should always be used;
