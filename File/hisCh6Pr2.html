History of the Empire - Chapter 6.2, Once again on the brink of war

In 214 p.I., an Aspakeian intelligence drone was captured deep in Lamnan territory. The drone carried highly classified data, including surveillance of Lamnan military bases and communications intercepts. Lamna accused Aspakei of violating the NDD, demanding reparations and threatening to withdraw from the Kadekei Accords. Aspakei denied the allegations, claiming the drone had been hijacked and redirected by private citizens. The INC launched an investigation, but before it could conclude, a Lamnan-backed militia bombed a key trade route in the Neutral Corridor, killing 15 and injuring almost 50. The bombing led to an emergency summit in the city of Varinozhkei. During the summit, President of Aspakei Telvoro Bikorbesto Lucozhkeif presented evidence of Lamna’s involvement in the bombing. Aspakei and Sark demanded punitive sanctions against Lamna, while Kojkos defended its ally, accused Aspakei of fabricating the evidence to justify its surveillance operations. The summit nearly collapsed when Lamna threatened to withdraw from the NDD entirely. Smaller states, fearing the collapse of the fragile peace, proposed a compromise with a new demilitarized and neutral zone. While the summit's compromise preserved the NDD, Lamna and Kojkos secretly entered into the Shadow Pact, a clandestine alliance aimed at undermining the republics. Aspakei's intelligence network uncovered hints of the Shadow Pact in 213 p.I., but concrete evidence was never found.

The delicate balance shattered in 211 p.I., when a skirmish broke out near Fort Xarno, a key checkpoint in the Neutral Corridor. Lamnan forces, disguised as local militias, attacked an Aspakeian convoy carrying supplies for the NZSF. Aspakeian troops responded, pushing the attackers back into Lamnan territory. Kojkos condemned Aspakei’s actions, claiming its forces had violated the demilitarized zone. The INC demanded an immediate cessation of hostilities and dispatched investigators to determine the cause of the attack. However, before the investigation could conclude, Lamnan state media broadcasted footage of Aspakeian soldiers allegedly looting a village near the border. The footage, later revealed to be doctored, caused outrage among Lamna’s population, leading to mass demonstrations demanding retaliation.

By 210 p.I., was once again on the edge of a war. Aspakei and Sark mobilized their forces, preparing for a potential invasion of the Neutral Corridor. Lamna and Kojkos began deploying troops to strategic locations along their borders. Despite the starting conflict, all sides hesitated to take the first step, knowing it would trigger the NDD’s mutual defence provisions and lead to widespread economic sanctions. The INC, desperate to prevent another war, proposed a radical solution; The INC presented what came to be known as the Grand Ultimatum:

1. Mandatory disarmament in contested regions, enforced by the NZSF; 2. All signatories must allow international observers access to military facilities to verify compliance; 3. Automatic economic sanctions against any nation proven to be funding or supporting militias.

Failure to comply would result in the suspension of the NDD, leaving the offending nation vulnerable to retaliation. The Grand Ultimatum created a stalemate. Neither side was willing to risk the economic and political fallout of being labelled the aggressor. Aspakei and Sark accepted the terms, while Lamna and Kojkos, under international pressure, agreed to scale back their covert operations. The Neutral Corridor, now heavily monitored by the NZSF, became a symbol of the region’s uneasy peace. Even though the Grand Ultimatum prevented a new war, the tensions remained unresolved on a deeper level.
