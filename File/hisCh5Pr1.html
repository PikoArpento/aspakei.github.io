History of the Empire - Chapter 5, The beginning of the Second Big War

By 230 p.I., The Republic of Sark, Republic of Mal, Reign of Kojkos, and the Land of Our God Lamna all vied for influence in the strategic central plains and resource-rich southern territories. Aspakei, still recovering from its internal crises, aligned itself firmly with Sark and Mal through the Council of Free States.

The Reign of Kojkos, a fiercely militarized theocracy with expansionist ambitions, had begun claiming neutral territories along its borders, particularly the contested Karno Valley. Meanwhile, the Land of Our God Lamna, an authoritarian state, declared its intent to "liberate" the region from what it saw as the corrupt influence of secular republics.

In 228 p.I., Lamna launched a surprise invasion of the Karno Valley, rapidly occupying key cities. Aspakei and Sark, bound by treaty, mobilized their forces to counter the aggression. Initially, the Republic of Mal also declared its support, deploying troops alongside Aspakeian and Sarkan force, but cracks in the alliance soon became evident. Mal’s ruling elite, driven by secret negotiations with Kojkos and promises of territorial expansion, began stalling its military operations. Mal’s betrayal was revealed in early 227 p.I., when its army retreated from a critical defensive line, leaving Aspakeian and Sarkan forces exposed. Within days, Mal declared its neutrality, citing "unjustified aggression" by Aspakei and Sark. The betrayal caused chaos on the battlefield, forcing Aspakei and Sark into a desperate retreat to avoid encirclement by Lamnan and Kojkan forces.

The Holy Siege of Port Karno in late 227 p.I. became the turning point. Aspakeian forces together with reinforcements from Sark, managed to hold the port for months despite relentless attacks.

General Keyko Mumurbesto Fluvyozhkeif devised a strategy to stabilize the front. Utilizing Aspakei’s advanced drone technology and fortified mountain defences, she ordered a tactical withdrawal to the Fyaska Valley, a region already fortified during the previous conflicts. Keyko’s forces launched a series of devastating counterattacks, disrupting Kojkan supply lines and slowing Lamna’s advance. Meanwhile, Sark, though weakened by Mal’s betrayal, reinforced Aspakei’s flanks, solidifying the defensive front.
