History of the Empire - Chapter 5.2, The Battle of Kadekei and the counterstrike

226 p.I., Lamnan forces launched a blitz toward Kadekei, aiming to decapitate its leadership and cripple morale. The attack caught Aspakei off-guard, forcing President Telvoro to evacuate to a secure location. However, Keyko, anticipating such a move, had prepared the capital with layers of defensive fortifications.

The Battle of Kadekei raged for weeks. Aspakeian forces eventually repelled the Lamnan assault, dealing heavy casualties to the invaders. The victory boosted Aspakei’s morale and solidifying Keyko’s reputation as a military genius.

By 225 p.I., Sark and Aspakei launched Steel Wings, a coordinated counteroffensive aimed at retaking the Karnis Valley and crippling Kojkan logistics. Utilizing Aspakei’s technological edge and Sark’s infantry, the allies achieved a series of victories, culminating in the liberation of Fort Kinaro.

Kojkos, now overstretched and facing growing dissent among its conscripted forces, began to fall. Meanwhile, Lamna, isolated by the defection of several smaller states to the Aspakeian-Sarkan alliance, found itself unable to maintain its offensive momentum.
