History of the Empire - Chapter 1.2, the coupe

On October 24, 399 BC, everything was ready. Three army battalions composed mainly of kondo people had been bribed, so at exactly midnight, the plan was put into action. Army hackers disabled the power and communications of the city, while men armed with small handguns entered the borders of the most important cities and walked towards the Royal Palace. At 0:20 the bomb squad planted fake C4 in important residential areas and near the Royal Palace, before moving away and calling emergency services. Meanwhile, a team of snipers positioned themselves on the roof of a building in front of the Palace. Ten minutes later the bomb threat was announced by the emergency services, and the King Protection Process was initiated. At 0:35 he evacuated his palace ccompanied by two convoys of bodyguards, but his car had only one flow: its tires were not bulletproof. A sniper fired, hitting a tire, causing the car to skid. The first rebel battallion approached, neutralizing the bodyguards with very few losses. At 1:14 the King was taken hostage and brought back to the royal palace.

At 1:50 the soldiers read live on national TV and on all radios the declaration of independence of the Kondo people, who had decided to call themselves "Aspakei". Immediately after, the King was executed and the rest of the army decided to intervene militarily, so the Minister of Defence, who acquired special powers in the absence of the King, declared war on the rebels. The first civil war officially began at 2:23 am on October 24, 399 p.I.
