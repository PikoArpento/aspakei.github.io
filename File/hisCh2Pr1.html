History of the Empire - Chapter 2, political and financial changes

First of all, it is decided to establish a senate composed of 100 members elected by the citizens to reform the newborn Republic. The city chosen to be home of the senate building is Isozhkei.The building costs over 48 million Taro, the most expensive building of all at the time, built entirely of blue ice, compact ice, and spruce wood, in the style of the city. It was built in the city centre, in front of the statue called “Bu Aspististoyo Kurko” making it one of the most important cities. Among the 100 senators are added the Cave Comrades, who perform a representative function, Xarg becomes ambassador in international conferences, Qi representative in internal meetings, while Somoa holds public speeches. On November 20, 297 BC, the first multi-party elections are held. Over 40 parties present themselves with almost 800 total participants, and after a week of elections, the senators of 10 different parties are chosen. In 292 BC, the construction of the Senate building is also completed.

The newly elected senators did not wait to propose and implement reforms, among which the most important from the end of the First Civil War to 390 BC are the establishment of the Police force, the drafting of the first constitution of Aspakei, and the birth of an early form of penal code based on the Kondo culture, with penalties ranging from fines to the death penalty the sentencing of all soldiers of the former Elu Kingdom and a mass amnesty for Aspakei war criminals. In addition, of laws in favour of the lower social class in order to disempower the rich and help the poorest imposition of compulsory education were imposed, and last, laws to protect labour, especially agriculture.

Another of the most important reforms was the introduction of a new currency, the Taro, more powerful than the previous one, the Din Elu, because it contained a greater quantity of emeralds inside the coins. Both coins and banknotes were created, and on January 1, 390 BC they were put into circulation. At the very beginning there here were 4 banknotes, 1 Taro, 2, 10 and 50. They were all of different colours, respectively red/orange, blue/light blue, purple/pink, green. On the front they had the value on the left, the symbol of the BCI, and on the right the symbol of the Taro. On the back instead they had the writing «K S A», which stands for «Kadojoi ke Senato Aspakeif», or «The People and the Senate of Aspakei». With the introduction of a new more powerful currency, the economy damaged by the First Civil War was reborn, the internal market was reborn and the export of agricultural materials resumed.

After the First Civil War, the most important cities were obviously devastated. Teams of volunteers immediately set to work to rebuild the cities, choosing a different theme for each one. The capital was then moved in 390 BC from Spursozhkei to Kadekei, right on Mount Xarg, both for its symbolic value and for its particular strategic position. Thus, Mount Xarg was terraformed to become habitable and Kadekei was built. This, together with the improvement of economy and labour, led to the drastic improvement of social conditions. Also, thanks to various reforms the crime rate was reduced and the rate of higher education increased, life expectancy increased, the currency gained purchasing power and consequently the economy of the state improved. Now no longer being subjected to another people, the Kondo stopped frequently revolting, which allowed them to reduce the military budget and invest that money in solving social problems. The quality of life took a huge step forward, and within a few decades Aspakei became one of the countries where people lived best.
