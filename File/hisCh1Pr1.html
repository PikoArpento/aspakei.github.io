History of the Empire - Chapter 1, before the indipendence

Before independence, the now people of The Empire of Aspakei were under the control of the Elu Kingdom, which controlled three large local cultures, their own, the Harmoni, and the Kondo, the main Aspakeian culture. Of these, the Kondos were the ones with the greatest desire for independence.

The situation reached its peak on May 6, 400 p.I., when a revolt was organized in the capital, and for the first time the government was forced to deploy the military and give them the order to shoot. This event is known today as "The revolt of May". Of nearly 1,200 protesters, over 300 were injured, and over 100 people were killed. Seeing the massacre, one of the protesters decided to act once and for all and free the Kondos. That boy, 21-year-old Xargur Mastor, hid himself in a mountain that now brings his name. At that time, that peak was quite inhospitable due to the extreme climate.

As a legend says, at this point Xarg's story became intertwined with that of three unfortunate explorers. Stranded by a storm, they found refuge in a vast cave within the mountain. However, their situation became critical when they ran out of food, and one of the three died of starvation. It was in those crucial days that Xargur entered the lives of the explorers Qi and Somoa, offering his help in exchange for sustenance. An alliance was born, as young Xargur joined the explorers to face the challenges of the mountain together. During their shared adventures, Xargur revealed to his companions his desire for revenge and his intention to plan an attack against the authorities who had repressed the Konda protest. So, in the year 400 p.I., between the mountain peaks of Xargur and the depths of the cave, the foundations were laid for a rebellion that would change the fate of Aspakei and lead to the birth of the People's Democratic Empire and the Aspakei Senate.

As the everlasting storm raged outside the cave, Xargur and the scouts, who now call themselves the "Cave Comrades," developed an unbreakable bond. As the days passed, the strategy for the assassination took shape. On July 15, 400 p.I., Xarg and his allies chose to call this pact " The Mountain Treaty." The Mountain Treaty stipulated that once independence was achieved, a nation would be founded based on the principles of democracy and popular participation. The Senate would be the pillar upon which the new nation would be built. The Cave Companions pledged their loyalty to each other, promising to continue their common cause until victory or death. Thus began the countdown to Aspakei Independence, a story of rebellion and determination that would leave an indelible mark on the history of the People's Democratic Empire and the Aspakei Senate.

More reliable sources say that Qi and Somoa were two military leaders, and Xargur passed his days by training with the corrupted military.
