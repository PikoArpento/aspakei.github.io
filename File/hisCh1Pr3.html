History of the Empire - Chapter 1.3, the First Civil War

As soon as the troops on both sides had time to organize, Aspakei tried to create a front that was as small as possible but at the same time allowed him to control the most important cities on Lake Spergoro. They opted for the Fyaska Valley, connected to the Kosm River, so that the front would reach the border and there would be no way around it. November was devoid of major battles, almost all of them were aggressions by Aspakei who was trying to Improve its own strategic situation.

In January 298 p.I., the cities in the Central Mountains were evacuated for fear of future rebel attacks. The Royal Army in the first months penetrated through the centre of the occupied territory in a new attempt to divide Aspakei. This time they actually entered several hundred kilometres, but they again suffered heavy defeats and were forced to retreat. Meanwhile, Aspakei exploited the lightly defended sides in an attempt to surround the Central Mountains and take control of them, but they too encountered too many difficulties and had to stop on the peaks of Mount Xarg. Meanwhile, the first negotiations began. The Cave Comrades asked for the total independence of the Kondo people, but the Kingdom refused.

On March 2, 397 BC, the Kingdom mounts its third major counteroffensive, again bombing and attempting to break the front to encircle the Rebels. They passed Mount Xarg and liberated most of the occupied areas of the Central Mountains plus the region northwest of the Kingdom. Realizing that this could be the end for all the Kondo people, the rebel army sends two diplomats to secretly negotiate with the powerful Republic of Tehera, which already recognizes their independence, for military support. In August 397 BC, the two states come to a deal: Tehera would declare war on the Kingdom, but would receive the coastal territories in the event of victory.

After reaching an agreement, the Republic of Tehera, one of the most powerful states, officially declares war on the Kingdom on August 16. The next day, it begins the mobilization of 250,000 men who start a new front in the south, forcing the Kingdom to redeploy troops. This action proves extremely effective and forces the Kingdom to make major retreats. Both factions have the ability to advance dozens of kilometres a day, marking the end of the Kingdom in a matter of months.

Between August and October 397 BC, the Kingdom suffered constant and heavy defeats, the worst of which was on September 1, 397 BC, in the city of Borvozhkei, the city where it all started. In 14 hours of fighting, over 12,000 lives were lost. Although the situation was serious on both sides, it was the royal army that retreated. In October 397 BC, the Kingdom reached its historical low, losing 90% of its total territory, however, the soldiers eager for revenge applied a resistance to the bitter end, damaging the rebel battalions.

On October 24, 397 BC, the Kingdom's armed forces took to the streets unarmed and waving spruce branches, a symbol of surrender. Aspakei arrested the military leaders and seized their weapons, while the Cave Comrades began negotiations with the Minister of Defence and an ambassador from Tehera. The division of the territory negotiated with the Republic of Tehera was accepted, the maritime area was left to them, the Central Mountains, inhabited mainly by Kondos, were made independent under the name of the Republic of Aspakei. Meanwhile, in the south, the Elu were stripped of almost all political rights, and a vassal government of Aspakei was imposed.
