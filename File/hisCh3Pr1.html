History of the Empire - Chapter 3, Political tensions raising

While Aspakei focused on rebuilding, rumours of unrest began to circulate within the borders. The Kingdom of Sark, an ally of Xam’apo, refused to acknowledge the Treaty of Zhero, deeming it a betrayal of Elu interests. Sark’s military started conducting drills dangerously close to Aspakei’s northern border, escalating tensions. In response, Aspakei reinforced its defenses and renewed military alliances with Tehera and Mal. At the same time, intelligence reports indicated signs of underground movements in Aspakei. Small but organized groups which embraced Xam’apo’s cause were found plotting acts of sabotage. These factions, later known as the “Ashen Accord”, executed several attacks on critical infrastructure, including a bombing of the Tehera-Aspakei trade railway in early 297 p.I.

By 295 p.I., Aspakei’s leadership experienced a significant transformation. President Paxur Rospur Isozhkeif, celebrated as a war hero, announced his retirement from politics. The subsequent election was characterized by a battle between two contrasting visions for Aspakei’s future: the progressist “Bu Sinoyo Soyno” led by Orinko Kelso Kadekeif, who advocated for greater integration of the Elu population and expanded diplomatic outreach, and the nationalist “Aspazhvindo” led by Torvoynko Pesho Borvozhkei, who called for a stronger military and stricter cultural policies. Orinko won by a narrow margin. Her administration launched ambitious reforms, including economic incentives for the regions most affected by the war and new trade agreements with neutral states. Orinko’s government also initiated a cultural exchange program, bringing together Aspakeian and Elu youth to promote understanding and unity.

Despite these initiatives, the threat of conflict remained present. In 293 p.I., reports emerged of Sark gathering troops along its southern border. Sark was also seen conducting naval manoeuvres in the Bay of Zhero. Tehera’s intelligence agents uncovered evidence that Sark was covertly funding the Ashen Accord, which further strained regional stability. Orinko’s administration worked to get the situation under control through diplomacy, appealing to neutral nations for mediation and pressuring the Kingdom of Sark with economic sanctions. These efforts got a result when an international summit in Mal resulted in the “Accord of Watchful Peace,” where Sark pledged to withdraw its forces and cease support for insurgents in exchange for Aspakei lifting certain sanctions.

By 290 p.I., the republic had rebuilt much of what the war had destroyed, with new industrial centres, revitalized agriculture, and a resurgent cultural identity. The reforms of Kelso Orinko’s government paved the way for a new generation of leaders dedicated to preserving peace and promoting progress. Although tensions with Xam’apo and Sark persisted, Aspakei’s alliances with Tehera and Mal remained strong. Together, the old Treaty of the Republics became the Council of Free States, a coalition committed to mutual defence and economic cooperation.

By 285 p.I. the fragile peace in Aspakei began to show signs of strain. Economic recovery, while significant, had created important disparities between urban centres and rural regions. Eventually, the strain reached the government. President Orinko faced increasing backlash from the Aspazhvindo, who claimed she was putting diplomacy ahead of national security. The Assembly of the Republic turned into a battleground, with discussions quickly escalating into heated arguments. In the escalating chaos, General Torvoynko, the leader of the Aspakeian Army and a celebrated hero from the First Big War, became a divisive figure. Once a supporter of the democratic system, Torvoynko began expressing his dissatisfaction with the government's perceived lack of action. In his public addresses, he cautioned that the nation's security was in jeopardy if “weakness and division” continued to hinder effective leadership. His appeal grew among both military personnel and civilians, as many viewed him as a source of stability during these uncertain times.
